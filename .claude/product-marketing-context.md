# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-10*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Personal portfolio and professional brand website for Davor Minchorov, a senior software engineer and contractor.
**What it does:** Serves as a professional hub showcasing client work across industries, establishing thought leadership in software architecture, and directing visitors to Codyssey.dev (educational platform). Functions as the central landing point for potential clients and collaborators.
**Product category:** Professional portfolio / personal brand site
**Product type:** Static website (Astro + Tailwind CSS, deployed on Cloudflare Pages)
**Business model:** Lead generation for contract software engineering services; secondary promotion of Codyssey.dev educational platform

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Startups, growth-stage companies, and Fortune 500 enterprises needing experienced software engineering talent for contract/project work
**Decision-makers:** CTOs, VPs of Engineering, hiring managers, product managers evaluating senior engineering hires
**Primary use case:** Finding a reliable, senior-level software engineer who can design systems, build full-stack solutions, and lead teams
**Jobs to be done:**
- Evaluate Davor's experience and technical capabilities before reaching out
- See proof of real-world project delivery across industries
- Understand the breadth of services (architecture, full-stack dev, team enablement)
**Use cases:**
- Startup needs a senior engineer to design and build their product from scratch
- Enterprise needs a contractor to lead a team on a complex project
- Company evaluating Davor for a long-term engineering partnership

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Companies struggle to find experienced software engineers who combine deep technical skills with business understanding and can deliver production-grade solutions independently.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Junior contractors need heavy oversight and produce fragile code
- Agencies are expensive and lack the deep ownership a dedicated engineer provides
- Full-time hires take months to recruit and onboard
**What it costs them:** Delayed product launches, technical debt, failed projects, wasted engineering budget
**Emotional tension:** Fear of hiring the wrong person and wasting months; anxiety about whether a contractor can truly own and deliver complex work

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other senior freelance/contract software engineers — often lack the breadth of industry experience (healthcare, fintech, gaming, non-profit) or the architecture-level thinking
**Secondary:** Staff augmentation agencies — more expensive, less personal ownership, rotating engineers
**Indirect:** Hiring full-time — slower, more expensive long-term commitment, harder to justify for project-based needs

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- 10+ years across startups to Fortune 500 (not just one niche)
- Full-stack + system architecture + team leadership (rare combination)
- Proven delivery across 6+ industries (healthcare, fintech, non-profit, gaming, entertainment, social)
- Educator mindset — improves teams and processes, not just code
**How we do it differently:** Partner with organizations rather than just execute tasks; bring architectural thinking and business impact focus to every engagement
**Why that's better:** Clients get a senior engineer who thinks like a CTO — someone who can design systems, ship features, mentor teams, and drive real business outcomes
**Why customers choose us:** Track record of delivering "game-changing features" that "drive real customer and business impact" (client words)

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Current engineer/agency isn't delivering quality; technical debt piling up; missed deadlines; need someone who can own the architecture
**Pull:** Proven track record across industries; testimonials from real clients; educator mindset means better long-term code
**Habit:** Comfort with existing team or agency; "we'll just hire full-time eventually"
**Anxiety:** "Will a remote contractor integrate with our team?"; "What if the engagement doesn't work out?"
